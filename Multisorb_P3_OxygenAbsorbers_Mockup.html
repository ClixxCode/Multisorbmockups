Preserve the Freshness of Your Products With Premium Oxygen Absorbers

Oxygen absorbers, also called oxygen scavengers, protect sensitive products by removing oxygen from the package to prevent spoilage and extend shelf life. Since 1961, Multisorb has engineered solutions that preserve quality, freshness, and safety across food, nutraceuticals, healthcare, and cannabis.

By controlling residual oxygen, they protect against oxidation, mold, and aerobic bacteria while maintaining potency, flavor, and texture. With expertly engineered oxygen sorbents and in-house simulation, Multisorb specifies the exact absorber your product and packaging need.

How Oxygen Absorbers Work

An oxygen absorber (or oxygen scavenger) chemically binds the oxygen inside a sealed package, keeping working long after the package is closed.

1

Reactive material

A sachet or component contains a reactive material, most commonly food-grade iron, sized to the package.

2

Oxygen is bound

As the iron oxidizes, it pulls residual oxygen out of the headspace and the product, typically below the level where oxidation, mold, and aerobic spoilage occur.

3

Protection over shelf life

Unlike gas flushing, the absorber keeps mopping up oxygen that permeates the film over the product's life. The two are often used together.

How to Choose the Right Oxygen Absorber

  • Residual oxygen target. Most food applications target below 0.1% residual oxygen; specify your threshold.
  • Product water activity. StabilOx performs even at low water activity, where standard iron-based absorbers struggle, which suits dry pharma and nutra products.
  • Package format and barrier. Container, pouch, or case-ready tray, and the film's oxygen transmission rate, drive absorber size.
  • MAP compatibility. Absorbers work alongside nitrogen flush and modified atmosphere packaging.

Frequently Asked Questions About Oxygen Absorbers

How should I store unused oxygen absorbers?
In an airtight container to prevent premature activation from exposure to air.
Are Multisorb's oxygen absorbers FDA compliant?
Yes. Multisorb's oxygen absorbers are FDA-compliant and meet safety and regulatory standards for use with food and healthcare products.
How do I know the oxygen absorbers are still effective?
Many include an indicator, such as a color change, to show whether they remain active.
What is the difference between an oxygen absorber and a desiccant?
An oxygen absorber removes oxygen; a desiccant removes moisture. Products prone to rancidity or aerobic spoilage need an absorber; products prone to caking or mold need a desiccant. Many need both.
Which oxygen absorber is best for food packaging?
FreshPax is engineered for food, with FreshPax CR for case-ready meat. The right size depends on your residual oxygen target, product water activity, and package barrier.
Do oxygen absorbers work with modified atmosphere packaging (MAP)?
Yes. Absorbers complement nitrogen flush and MAP by removing the oxygen that remains after sealing and that permeates the film over shelf life.
Can I buy oxygen absorbers in bulk or wholesale?
Multisorb is a manufacturer and supplier of oxygen absorbers for commercial and industrial volumes. Contact us for bulk and custom orders.
What oxygen level do absorbers achieve?
Most food applications target below 0.1% residual oxygen. Multisorb sizes the absorber to your specific target and packaging.
