What Is a Desiccant Canister?

A desiccant canister is a rigid, drop-in sorbent unit inserted into a bottle or container before capping. Unlike loose desiccant or sachets, the canister keeps the sorbent fully contained, so it controls headspace humidity without dusting or migrating into the product.

Canisters are the standard moisture control format for high-speed bottle filling lines. They drop into the container through automated dispensing equipment at the filler, ride with the product through its shelf life, and protect against the moisture-driven failure modes each industry fights: potency loss and degradation in oral solid dose drugs, caking and viability loss in supplements, and chemistry interference in diagnostic test kits.

How to Choose a Desiccant Canister

Start with three questions: what is your container and headspace volume, what moisture condition does your product need across its shelf life, and how will the canister be inserted on your line. Those three answers narrow the format and size quickly, and Multisorb's packaging engineers can validate the selection with simulation before you commit.

Size: 1g, 2g, or 3g

Canister size is driven by headspace volume, the moisture sensitivity of the product, target shelf life, and the barrier performance of the container. Rather than oversizing by guesswork, Multisorb can model your exact container, fill count, and climate zone through packaging simulation and recommend the smallest canister that holds your required headspace RH for the full shelf-life period.

Traditional Canister or Compressed Solid Form

If dusting risk, plastic reduction, or space constraints are driving the evaluation, compressed desiccant canisters carry twice the capacity per unit volume and remove loose fill from the equation entirely. If you are qualifying against an established loose-fill specification, SorbiCap integrates without process change.

Canister or Another Format

Canisters suit rigid containers on automated lines. If your packaging is a flexible pouch, blister, or tight-geometry device, a packet or another desiccant format may fit better. For the full trade-off analysis, see our guide on resin vs canister sorbents.

Moisture Plus Oxygen

Oxidation-sensitive products such as probiotics often need dual protection. Multisorb pairs desiccant canisters with StabilOx oxygen absorber canisters for combined moisture and oxygen control in a single bottle.

Desiccant Canister FAQs

What sizes do desiccant canisters come in?
Multiform CSF canisters are available in 1g, 2g, and 3g sizes, the standard insert sizes for pharmaceutical and supplement bottles. Will Multiform CSF canisters run on our existing dispensing equipment?
Yes. Multiform CSF is designed to run on existing canister dispensing equipment with no retooling, so switching from a loose-fill canister does not require changes to your filling line. That compatibility is central to keeping the change-control burden of a format switch manageable.
What type of plastic are the canisters made with?
Multisorb's desiccant canisters are crafted using durable, FDA-compliant plastics, such as high-density polyethylene (HDPE). These materials are safe, non-toxic, and suitable for pharmaceutical, nutraceutical, and diagnostic applications. Multiform CSF differs by design: it is a compressed solid form unit that replaces the filled plastic shell entirely, using 65-85% less plastic per unit.
